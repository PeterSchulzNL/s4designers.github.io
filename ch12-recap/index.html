Chapter 12: recap: let's review what you've learned by now

You've discovered quite a few new concepts, and some programming jargon. Most of these ideas are related and connected in some way. Now, would you like to read a long and abstract essay about the important programming concepts and their relations? If not, you're in luck. We are going to work with concept maps to do a recap and summarize of what you should understand by now. Concept maps are more visual, and a very effective notation for learning and communicating the main points of study material.

12.1 another concept: parameters

  • Add two concepts to the concept map: parameters and arguments.
  • Connect the new concepts to the rest of the map. What are the most important concepts to connect to? You should be able to make at least four connections. Six is better.
  • Give the connections meaningful labels. Think about the direction of the connection-arrow.

To send in your work, create a JPEG or a PNG image from your concept map, and mail the image.

Use the menu: File ▸ Export CMap As ▸ Image File…, then choose JPEG or PNG as the file format, and save.

12.2 side effects

  • Add a connection between the concept side effect and either local variable or global variable. Which one do you choose? Give the connection an informative label.
  • Add two more examples of side effects, that are specific to JavaScript, to the concept map. Can you think of Javascript side-effects that are not about changing the web page?
  • Add one more example of a side effect that is specific to Arduino. Can you think of Arduino side effects that are not about changing an output pin?

12.4 more about operators

There is just a single concept for operator on the map.

  • create a connection that starts at operator, and points to value. What is the best label for that connection?
  • create the reverse connection: from value to operator. What is the best label for that connection? Feel free to revise the label for the other connection. They have to make sense together.
  • add concepts for the three important families of operators, and add two examples of each family.
  • can you create some (say 3 or 4) connections from these operator families to other parts of the concept map?
